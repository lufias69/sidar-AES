# Assessing Writing - Journal Submission Guidelines Summary

**Journal:** Assessing Writing (Elsevier)  
**ISSN:** 1075-2935  
**Publisher:** Elsevier  
**Submission Portal:** https://www.editorialmanager.com/asw/

---

## Key Journal Information

### Scope
Assessing Writing is a refereed international journal providing a forum for ideas, research and practice on the assessment of written language. It publishes articles, book reviews, conference reports, and academic exchanges concerning writing assessments of all kinds, including traditional (direct and standardized forms of) testing of writing, alternative performance assessments (such as portfolios), workplace sampling, testing of second language writing, and classroom-based assessment.

### Article Types
- **Full Research Articles:** 7,000-10,000 words (including references)
- **Brief Reports:** 3,000-4,000 words
- **Review Articles:** Up to 12,000 words
- **Commentaries:** 2,000-3,000 words

### Manuscript Format (Standard Elsevier Requirements)

#### 1. Title Page
- Title (concise, max 150 characters)
- Author names and affiliations
- Corresponding author email
- Present/permanent address if different
- Word count (excluding title page, abstract, references, tables, figures)

#### 2. Abstract
- 150-250 words
- Structured or unstructured (check recent issues)
- No citations

#### 3. Keywords
- 4-6 keywords
- Not already in title

#### 4. Manuscript Structure
**For Empirical Studies:**
1. Introduction (no heading)
2. Literature Review / Theoretical Framework
3. Research Questions
4. Method
   - Participants
   - Materials/Instruments
   - Procedures
   - Data Analysis
5. Results
6. Discussion
7. Conclusion/Implications
8. Limitations
9. References

#### 5. References
- APA 7th Edition style
- DOIs required where available
- All references cited in text must be in reference list and vice versa

#### 6. Tables and Figures
- Numbered consecutively
- Cited in text
- Captions below figures, above tables
- Editable format (not images for tables)
- High resolution (minimum 300 dpi) for figures

#### 7. Supplementary Materials
- Accepted and encouraged
- Data files, additional analyses, instruments, code
- Uploaded separately during submission

## Submission Requirements

### Mandatory Files
✅ **Title Page** (separate file with author information)  
✅ **Blinded Manuscript** (no author identifying information)  
✅ **Abstract** (as part of manuscript, 150-250 words)  
✅ **Highlights** (3-5 bullet points, 85 characters each)  
✅ **Graphical Abstract** (optional but recommended)

### Declarations Required
✅ **Funding Sources**  
✅ **Conflict of Interest Statement**  
✅ **Data Availability Statement**  
✅ **Ethical Approval** (if human subjects)  
✅ **Informed Consent** (if applicable)

### Optional but Recommended
- **Supplementary Materials** (datasets, code, extended tables)
- **Author Contributions Statement** (CRediT taxonomy)
- **Acknowledgments**

## Formatting Specifications

### Text Format
- **Font:** Times New Roman, 12pt
- **Spacing:** Double-spaced throughout
- **Margins:** 2.54 cm (1 inch) all sides
- **Line Numbers:** Continuous
- **Page Numbers:** Bottom center
- **Alignment:** Left-aligned (not justified)
- **File Format:** MS Word (.docx) or LaTeX

### Headings
- **Level 1:** Bold, Title Case
- **Level 2:** Bold, Sentence case
- **Level 3:** Italic, Sentence case
- **Level 4:** Italic, indented

### Tables
- Editable (not images)
- Simple format (minimal lines)
- Notes below table using superscript letters

### Figures
- Separate files (TIFF, EPS, or high-res PNG)
- Minimum 300 dpi
- Color figures accepted (free online, charges for print)

## Submission Process

### Step 1: Prepare Manuscript
1. Format according to journal template
2. Create blinded version (remove author info)
3. Prepare title page separately
4. Create highlights (3-5 points)

### Step 2: Register Account
- https://www.editorialmanager.com/asw/
- Create account with corresponding author email

### Step 3: Complete Submission Form
- Article type
- Title and abstract
- Keywords
- Suggested reviewers (optional, 3-5)
- Exclude reviewers (if applicable)

### Step 4: Upload Files
1. Title page (with author info)
2. Blinded manuscript
3. Figures (if separate)
4. Supplementary materials
5. Data availability statement
6. Conflict of interest statement

### Step 5: Review and Submit
- Check all files uploaded correctly
- Review metadata
- Submit

## Review Process

### Timeline
- **Initial Review:** 1-2 weeks (desk review by editor)
- **Peer Review:** 6-10 weeks (2-3 reviewers)
- **Decision:** 8-12 weeks from submission
- **Revision:** 4-8 weeks after revision submitted

### Possible Decisions
- **Accept:** Rare on first submission
- **Minor Revisions:** Good fit, small issues
- **Major Revisions:** Good potential, significant improvements needed
- **Reject & Resubmit:** Substantial work required, can resubmit
- **Reject:** Not suitable for journal

## Tips for Assessing Writing Specifically

### What Editors Look For
✅ Rigorous research design  
✅ Clear relevance to writing assessment  
✅ Practical implications for educators  
✅ Methodological innovation or rigor  
✅ International applicability  
✅ Ethical considerations in assessment

### Common Rejection Reasons
❌ Insufficient sample size without justification  
❌ Lack of validity evidence  
❌ Limited discussion of assessment implications  
❌ Purely technical without educational context  
❌ Poor English language quality  
❌ Not within journal scope

### Recent Trends (2023-2025)
- Automated writing evaluation (AWE) systems
- AI and machine learning in assessment
- Validity and fairness in automated scoring
- Teacher and student perspectives on AWE
- Cross-linguistic writing assessment

## Checklist Before Submission

### Content
- [ ] Research questions clearly stated
- [ ] Method described in sufficient detail for replication
- [ ] Validity evidence for measures/instruments
- [ ] Results directly address research questions
- [ ] Discussion interprets findings in context of literature
- [ ] Practical implications for writing assessment clearly stated
- [ ] Limitations acknowledged
- [ ] Ethical considerations addressed

### Format
- [ ] Word count within limits (7,000-10,000)
- [ ] Abstract 150-250 words
- [ ] 4-6 keywords
- [ ] APA 7th edition references
- [ ] Tables/figures numbered and cited
- [ ] Line numbers included
- [ ] Double-spaced, 12pt Times New Roman

### Files
- [ ] Title page (separate, with author info)
- [ ] Blinded manuscript (no author identifying info)
- [ ] Figures (separate, high resolution)
- [ ] Supplementary materials prepared
- [ ] All declarations completed

### Legal/Ethical
- [ ] Institutional ethics approval obtained
- [ ] Data availability statement prepared
- [ ] Conflict of interest declared
- [ ] Permissions for copyrighted material
- [ ] Author contributions specified

### Production Process
1. **Proofs:** 5-10 days to review and return corrected proofs
2. **Copyright Transfer:** Sign copyright transfer agreement
3. **Open Access:** Optional (fee-based), decide at acceptance
4. **Publication:** Online first within 4-6 weeks, print issue within 3-6 months
