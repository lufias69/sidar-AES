## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### âŒ MISTAKE 1: Leaving "We" Everywhere
**Problem:** Overuse of "we" makes it clear authors are describing own work  
**Solution:** Use third person or passive voice in blinded version

### âŒ MISTAKE 2: Obvious Self-Citation Pattern
**Problem:** 8 out of 10 intro citations are "Smith et al." where Smith is author  
**Solution:** Include more diverse citations, don't bunch self-citations

### âŒ MISTAKE 3: Too-Specific Context
**Problem:** "One of only two programs in Southeast Asia offering..."  
**Solution:** Be more general - "A program in Southeast Asia"

### âŒ MISTAKE 4: File Metadata
**Problem:** File properties show "Author: John Smith, Company: XYZ University"  
**Solution:** File â†’ Info â†’ Remove All Personal Information

### âŒ MISTAKE 5: Acknowledgments in Blinded Version
**Problem:** "We thank Dr. Jane Doe from our department..."  
**Solution:** Remove entire Acknowledgments section from blinded version
